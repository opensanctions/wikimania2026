# Who is running the world? Political data matters

Slides for Ada Homolova and Johan Schuijt's Wikimania 2026 session about
[EveryPolitician](https://everypolitician.org/), Wikidata, and
[PoliLoom](https://loom.everypolitician.org/) — and why nobody knows who runs
the world, yet.

There is no complete, up-to-date list of the world's politicians. Records are
scattered across thousands of governments, in every language and format, and
they change constantly. We're fixing that by making Wikidata the definitive
source on who holds power — and we need your help.

## Help out

- **Verify data with PoliLoom** — our tool shows politician data extracted
  from the web, each claim backed by a verbatim quote from the source. You
  confirm or reject it; confirmed edits go to Wikidata under _your_ account.
  → [loom.everypolitician.org](https://loom.everypolitician.org/)
- **Edit Wikidata** — join the
  [WikiProject](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Wikidata:WikiProject_every_politician) or
  help [GovDirectory](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Wikidata:WikiProject_Govdirectory) map the world's governments.
- **Write code** — [github.com/opensanctions/poliloom](https://github.com/opensanctions/poliloom/)
  and the [crawler planning repo](https://github.com/opensanctions/crawler-planning).
- **Talk to us** — [discuss.opensanctions.org](https://discuss.opensanctions.org/).
  What worked, what didn't, what would make you come back?

See [everypolitician.org/about/contribute](https://everypolitician.org/about/contribute/)
for the full picture.
