Why political data matters

But nobody can keep this up alone

  • mySociety built EveryPolitician — paused 2019, unsustainable on grant funding
  • CIA closed the World Factbook in 2026 — without warning

Even the CIA can't maintain a reference database. A single funded team can't either.

Ada, 3 min. This is THE argument for community. Hands over to Johan.

EveryPolitician, revived

  • ~700,000 political office-holders
  • ~170,000 positions
  • 261 countries and territories
Johan, 2 min. Check current numbers before the talk.

The bet: Wikidata

  • Community-maintained — outlives any single team
  • Free, multilingual, unique identifiers
  • Already the backbone of EveryPolitician
Johan, 3 min.

The goal: commoditize political data

Make Wikidata the definitive source for who holds power — free for everyone.

Johan, 2 min.

How we get there

  1. Capture the web (evidence archived)
  2. Machines extract candidate facts
  3. Humans verify
  4. Confirmed data flows to Wikidata
Johan, 3 min. Keep light — one slide, no architecture. Machines do drudge work, humans make the call.

PoliLoom

  • Shows a claim + the exact source passage it came from
  • You confirm or reject
  • Confirmed edits go to Wikidata under your account
  • No Wikidata expertise needed
